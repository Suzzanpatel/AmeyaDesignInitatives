Elegantly Eclectic

Interior Designing

Scope

Interior Designing

Style

We designed keeping in mind.

The foyer has a comfortable seating on the outside and a smart way of hiding the electrical wire duct passing just outside their house, in a way that it can be accessed, but doesn't change the look of their foyer space. Since the client was fond of greenery, we made sure to inculcate a few shelves outside where they can keep plants & create a positive space right at the entrance.

They wanted to bring with them a few memories of their old home, the Hanuman frame in the foyer being one of those! We looked into the spaces and made sure that we create it without the constraints and unwillingness of the residents, as we made sure to incorporate it in our design.

Colours unfold as soon as you enter into the home. There is the use of bold yet subtle colours that are soothing to the eye and portray a hint of coolness and calm.

Our clients requested there to be a comfortable lounge chair, a recliner and a sofa and we made sure to inculcate all the different seating types in one room. We have looked into the anthropometry and comfort of the individual at a great level because they were sure that a major part of their time would be spent on these sofas.

The TV Panel, adorned with tiles and rose gold trims, is the statement of this room. To compliment this, we added rose gold in the coffee table as well as the sofa legs.

Such minute details are what make a huge difference to the MOOD OF A ROOM. The ceiling is specifically designed to give a soft & expansive look to the room. We achieved that with a use "soft box" also perfect lighting in the room.

The residents love to start their day with a filter coffee, and we made sure to provide them with a space dedicated for their coffee maker and all equipment that comes with it.

The purpose of the kitchen is giving the users a user-friendly and ergonomically designed space. With the coffee machine as a central element, we designed the kitchen to be beautiful and convenient.

The client is a macrame artist and required a rod to easily work on her pieces, and we fulfilled that requirement by inculcating the rod in a shelf design that served the purpose.

The folding table underneath the shelf makes the room look bigger when not in use & serves the purpose whenever required.

The use of cane laminate in their bed complements the entire look and bound the room together. It perfectly reflected the theme of the room and the client's taste.

The daughter's room was designed with a perfect blend of functionality and aesthetics. The study table was designed in a way that it could be used for both studying and as a dressing unit. The wardrobe was designed with ample storage space and a mirror unit.

The bed design incorporates storage underneath, maximizing the space utility. The color scheme was kept soft and soothing, creating a perfect environment for both study and rest.
