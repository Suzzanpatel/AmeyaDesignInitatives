Shank by Kalapurna

Boutique Interior Renovation

Scope

Interior & Spatial Design

Location

Commercial Complex

Innovative ceiling design featuring flowing fabric-like patterns in pastel colors, creating a modern and dynamic atmosphere.

The whole design of the sign board was formulated based on the location of the showroom. The complex have shops in a street style, with rows of shops on either side, so for our board to stand out and to be visible from all sides the design was arrived at.

The client wanted a modern chic showroom to outstand the rest in the complex. The design idea was to SELL FABRIC. So the whole design was oriented towards this purpose. The grey, white and natural tones was adopted as the fabrics in display stand out in these colours. Also, the ceiling in painted grey and lighting is exposed to give it a modern look.

Another display is made of natural wood and openings designed to help with stacking fabrics easily. As we proceed in the showroom, another display with Jharokha style is incorporated to hang boutique related finished stitched products with lovely lighting and mirror for reflection.

For the boutique section the client wanted a loathak style design for better comfort as she needs to go through long hours of sitting for her style of work. Behind the wall is the impression of an artist for fashion which is monochromatic in colour over grey background.

The fabric flow false ceiling in this entrance gives the idea of flow of waves which really soothes the viewer on entry. A biophilic concept of design blended into the interior.

The cashier table is kept small & chic, with all basic requirements integrated in design. The counter top is made of white lacquered glass making it easy to display the coloured fabric and enhancing the product.

The use of grey stucco on wall and rose gold logo gives a simplistic feel and elegance behind the cashier.
